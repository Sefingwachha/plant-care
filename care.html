Plant Care

Care Guide
Light

Light is an important variable when it comes to keeping your Air Plant healthy. We recommend placing your Air Plant near a window with direct sunlight.

Pet Friendly

Water

Most Air Plants will be their healthiest with a weekly watering. Air Plants will become dry while in the terrarium.

Humidity

Air is an important component of the health of an Air Plant. All Air Plants live off the air and will be healthier in a terrarium that is not enclosed.

Most Common Question

Watering frequency depends on the type of plant, the season, and the environment. Generally, allow the top inch of soil to dry out between waterings for most indoor plants. Succulents and cacti require less frequent watering, while tropical plants may need more consistent moisture.

Signs of too much light include scorched or faded leaves, while too little light can cause yellowing leaves and leggy growth. Ensure you understand the specific light requirements of your plant and place it accordingly.

Use well-draining potting soil. For succulents and cacti, use a cactus mix. For other houseplants, a general indoor plant mix works well. Orchids and other specific plants may require specialized soil.

Fertilize during the growing season (spring and summer) every 2-4 weeks with a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer. Reduce or stop fertilizing in the fall and winter when plant growth slows down.
